# Movicol® (Macrogol 3350) — Prescribing Guide

**Formulation:** Each sachet (13.8 g powder) dissolved in 125 ml water
**Class:** Osmotic laxative (macrogol 3350 + balanced electrolytes)
**Electrolytes per sachet:** Na 65 mmol/L · K 5.4 mmol/L · Cl 53 mmol/L · HCO₃ 17 mmol/L

---

## STEP 1 — Identify the Indication

| Indication | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Chronic constipation** | Infrequent or difficult bowel movements; ongoing need |
| **Faecal impaction** | Refractory constipation with faecal loading of rectum/colon — **confirm by physical or radiological examination** |

---

## STEP 2 — Check Contraindications First

**Do NOT use Movicol if:**
- Intestinal perforation or obstruction (structural or functional)
- Ileus
- Severe inflammatory bowel disease: active Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, toxic megacolon
- Hypersensitivity to macrogol 3350 or any excipient

---

## STEP 3 — Dose by Indication and Patient

### 🟦 Chronic Constipation

| Population | Dose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Adults, adolescents, elderly** | **1–3 sachets daily** in divided doses | Max 2 weeks per course; can repeat |
| Extended use (MS, Parkinson's, opioid/antimuscarinic-induced) | Adjust down to **1–2 sachets daily** | Extended use permitted in these groups |
| **Children <12 years** | ❌ Not recommended — use Movicol Paediatric products | — |

> Clinical studies show many patients respond to 1–2 sachets/day; dose typically reduces over time as stool consistency normalises.

---

### 🟥 Faecal Impaction

| Population | Dose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Adults, adolescents, elderly** | **8 sachets daily**, all consumed within a **6-hour period** | Max **3 days** |
| **Impaired cardiovascular function** | Max **2 sachets per hour** (divide the 8 sachets over the day) | Max 3 days |
| **Renal insufficiency** | No dose adjustment needed | — |
| **Children <12 years** | ❌ Not recommended — use Movicol Paediatric products | — |

> **Preparation tip:** For faecal impaction, all 8 sachets may be dissolved together in **1 litre of water**.

---

## STEP 4 — Administration Instructions

- **Standard:** Dissolve each sachet in **125 ml water**
- **Faecal impaction:** Dissolve 8 sachets in **1 litre water**
- Reconstituted solution must be used within **6 hours** (store at 2–8°C if not used immediately)
- Sachets: store below 25°C

---

## STEP 5 — Special Populations

| Population | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Renal insufficiency** | No dose adjustment needed for either indication |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | For faecal impaction: max 2 sachets/hour (fluid load concern) |
| **Pregnancy** | Limited human data; animal studies show reproductive toxicity — use with caution, weigh risk/benefit |
| **Breastfeeding** | Safe — systemic absorption of macrogol 3350 is negligible |
| **Driving / machinery** | No effect |

---

## STEP 6 — Warnings to Monitor

| Warning | Action |
|---|---|
| Fluid/electrolyte shifts (oedema, dyspnoea, fatigue, cardiac failure) | **Stop immediately** + check electrolytes |
| Diarrhoea | Reduce dose — usually resolves |
| Hyperkalaemia or hypokalaemia | Electrolyte monitoring if prolonged use or high-dose |

---

## Drug Interactions

- **Macrogol raises solubility** of alcohol-soluble, water-insoluble drugs → altered absorption possible
- **Transit rate increase** may transiently reduce absorption of co-administered drugs
- **Anti-epileptics:** isolated reports of reduced efficacy — monitor levels
- **General rule:** space other oral medications away from Movicol doses where possible

---

## Overdose

- Severe pain or distension → nasogastric aspiration
- Extensive fluid/electrolyte loss → correct electrolyte disturbances

---

## Key Adverse Effects

| System | Effect |
|---|---|
| GI (most common) | Abdominal pain, diarrhoea, nausea, vomiting, bloating, flatulence, anal discomfort |
| Electrolytes | Hyperkalaemia, hypokalaemia |
| Immune | Allergic reactions (rare): anaphylaxis, angioedema, rash, urticaria |
| General | Peripheral oedema, headache |

> Mild diarrhoea usually responds to dose reduction.

---

## Clinical Guardrails

- **Always confirm faecal impaction** by physical or radiological exam before using the high-dose regimen
- **Cardiovascular patients on impaction dose:** must limit to 2 sachets/hour — rapid fluid load risk
- **Do not use in IBD flares or obstruction** — can cause perforation
- **Extended use is acceptable** for opioid/antimuscarinic-induced constipation and neurological disease (MS, Parkinson's) — adjust dose to minimum effective
- **Pregnancy:** caution warranted despite minimal absorption — animal reproductive toxicity data exist
