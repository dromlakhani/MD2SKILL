# Assessment and treatment of lipids and cardiovascular risk factors in early menopause

## STEP 1 — Gather Information
Collect menopause status (<45 years, amenorrhea, elevated FSH), lipid panel (total cholesterol, LDL-C, HDL-C, TG), traditional CV risk factors (age, smoking, blood pressure, diabetes), and risk-enhancing factors (family history of premature ASCVD, elevated Lp(a), etc.). Then calculate 10-year ASCVD risk using Pooled Cohort Equations.

## STEP 2 — Rule In / Rule Out
Is the woman confirmed to be in early menopause (<45 years) with amenorrhea and elevated FSH? If yes, proceed to risk stratification; if no, evaluate other causes of lipid abnormalities and manage per general lipid guidelines.

## STEP 3 — Classify or Stratify
Classify 10-year ASCVD risk: low (<5%), borderline (5-7.4%), intermediate (7.5-19.9%), high (≥20%). Consider risk-enhancing factors (Lp(a) ≥50 mg/dL, family history, etc.) and CAC scoring if risk is borderline/intermediate and uncertain. Designate as statin candidate if risk ≥7.5% or risk 5-7.4% with ≥1 risk-enhancing factor or CAC >0.

## STEP 4 — Decide
If statin candidate, initiate moderate- or high-intensity statin based on risk level plus lifestyle modification; if not statin candidate, emphasize lifestyle measures and repeat lipid panel and risk assessment in 3-5 years.

## Clinical Guardrails / Mimics / Pitfalls
Do not use hormone therapy solely for lipid lowering; statins are preferred over HT for CVD risk reduction in early menopause. Avoid statin initiation in pregnancy or if planning pregnancy. Monitor for statin-associated muscle symptoms. Do not rely only on LDL-C targets without overall risk assessment; consider CAC when risk estimate is uncertain. Pitfalls: missing secondary causes like hypothyroidism, overlooking Lp(a) as a risk enhancer, and failing to recalculate risk after menopause.

## Concrete Clinical Example
A 42-year-old woman with 6 months of amenorrhea, FSH 45 mIU/L, LDL-C 138 mg/dL, HDL-C 45 mg/dL, TG 120 mg/dL, non-smoker, BP 118/70, no diabetes. 10-year ASCVD risk = 6.2% (borderline). She has a family history of premature MI in her father at age 48 (risk-enhancing factor). CAC score = 0. Decision: statin not generally recommended; emphasize lifestyle, repeat lipid panel and risk assessment in 3-5 years.

**Source:** Lipid Management in Patients with Endocrine Disorders: An Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline, Endocrine Society, 2020, doi:10.1210/clinem/dgaa674
