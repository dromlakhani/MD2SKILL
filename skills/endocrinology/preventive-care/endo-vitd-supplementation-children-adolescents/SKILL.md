# Vitamin D supplementation for children and adolescents

## STEP 1 — Gather Information
Collect patient age (1-18 years) and confirm absence of established indications for vitamin D treatment or 25(OH)D testing (e.g., no known rickets, malabsorption, chronic kidney disease, granulomatous diseases, or other conditions altering vitamin D physiology).

## STEP 2 — Rule In / Rule Out
If age is between 1 and 18 years inclusive and there are no established indications for vitamin D treatment or testing → proceed to Step 3; otherwise → do not suggest empiric vitamin D supplementation (follow age-appropriate Dietary Reference Intakes or evaluate for specific indications).

## STEP 3 — Classify or Stratify
Classify the patient as eligible for empiric vitamin D supplementation; no further stratification by age, sex, or baseline 25(OH)D level is required per guideline.

## STEP 4 — Decide
Prescribe empiric vitamin D supplementation via daily intake of fortified foods, vitamin D–containing formulations (pill or drops), or a vitamin D supplement; typical dosing ranges from 300 to 2000 IU daily, with an estimated weighted average of ~1200 IU per day.

## Clinical Guardrails / Mimics / Pitfalls
Do not rely on serum 25(OH)D testing to initiate or guide empiric supplementation in this population; avoid high intermittent bolus doses without monitoring for hypercalcemia; do not use in infants <1 year (guideline excludes this age group); avoid in patients with conditions that markedly alter vitamin D metabolism (e.g., chronic kidney disease, sarcoidosis) unless under specialist supervision; do not exceed the tolerable upper intake level without monitoring; do not replace dietary vitamin D or safe sun exposure with supplementation alone.

## Concrete Clinical Example
A 12-year-old with limited outdoor activity and no chronic illnesses presents for well-child visit; parent asks if vitamin D supplementation is needed. Age 12 years, no established indications → suggest empiric vitamin D supplementation (e.g., 600–1000 IU daily via fortified milk or supplement).
