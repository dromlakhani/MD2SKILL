# Interpret triglycerides for dyslipidemia screening in children 10-19 years

## STEP 1 — Gather Information
Obtain fasting triglyceride level, patient age (10-19 years), BMI percentile, and confirm fasting state (≥8 hours). End with action: proceed to triglyceride interpretation.

## STEP 2 — Rule In / Rule Out
Is fasting triglyceride ≥130 mg/dL? If yes, rule in high triglycerides and proceed to Step 4; if no, proceed to Step 3.

## STEP 3 — Classify or Stratify
Is fasting triglyceride 90-129 mg/dL? If yes, classify as borderline high; if <90 mg/dL, classify as acceptable. End with action: proceed to Step 4.

## STEP 4 — Decide
For acceptable triglycerides (<90 mg/dL): routine lipid screening per obesity guidelines; for borderline high (90-129 mg/dL): intensify lifestyle counseling, repeat fasting lipid panel in 12 months; for high triglycerides (≥130 mg/dL): obtain full lipid panel (LDL, HDL, total cholesterol), initiate structured lifestyle intervention, repeat lipids in 3-6 months, consider pharmacotherapy if LDL remains high after lifestyle.

## Clinical Guardrails / Mimics / Pitfalls
Do not interpret non-fasting triglyceride levels; avoid misclassifying due to recent illness, alcohol, or estrogen therapy; do not initiate pharmacotherapy based solely on triglycerides without evaluating LDL; remember that isolated high triglycerides may reflect genetic causes (e.g., familial hypertriglyceridemia) requiring specialist referral.

## Concrete Clinical Example
A 14-year-old girl with BMI at the 98th percentile presents for obesity screening; fasting triglycerides are 145 mg/dL. Classified as high triglycerides, full lipid panel shows LDL 130 mg/dL, HDL 35 mg/dL. Lifestyle intervention (diet, activity) initiated; lipids repeated at 4 months show triglycerides 110 mg/dL, LDL 110 mg/dL.

**Source:** Pediatric Obesity Assessment, Treatment, and Prevention: An Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline, Dennis M. Styne et al., 2017, doi:10.1210/jc.2016-2573
