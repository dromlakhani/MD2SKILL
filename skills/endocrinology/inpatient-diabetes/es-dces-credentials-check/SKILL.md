# Verify diabetes care and education specialist holds appropriate credentials or is working toward certification

## STEP 1 — Gather Information
Identify the DCES responsible for inpatient diabetes education and collect their current credentials (CDE, BC-ADM) or enrollment status in a recognized certification program.

## STEP 2 — Rule In / Rule Out
Is the DCES certified (CDE or BC-ADM) or actively pursuing certification? If yes, proceed; if no, flag a credential gap.

## STEP 3 — Classify or Stratify
- Certified: Fully compliant  
- Working toward certification: Conditionally compliant (verify progress and supervision)  
- Neither: Non‑compliant  

## STEP 4 — Decide
- Certified: Approve independent delivery of inpatient diabetes education.  
- Working toward: Permit education only under direct supervision of a credentialed DCES and require documented progress toward certification.  
- Non‑compliant: Reassign education duties to a credentialed DCES or delay education until appropriate credentials are obtained.

## Clinical Guardrails / Mimics / Pitfalls
Do not allow uncredentialed staff to provide independent diabetes education without supervision; do not assume general nursing staff can substitute for a DCES; do not overlook the need for ongoing competency verification even after initial certification.

## Concrete Clinical Example
A hospital nurse without DCES credentials is asked to provide inpatient diabetes education; review shows they are enrolled in a BC-ADM program with expected completion in 6 months. They are permitted to co‑educate with a certified DCES while completing their training, with supervision documented per shift.

**Source:** Management of Hyperglycemia in Hospitalized Adult Patients in Non-Critical Care Settings: An Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline, Endocrine Society, 2022, https://doi.org/10.1210/clinem/dgac278
