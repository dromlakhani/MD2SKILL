# Recommend against routine testosterone measurement for diagnosis

## STEP 1 — Gather Information
Document presenting symptoms (fatigue, low libido, mood changes) and screen for known endocrine disorders (hypopituitarism, adrenal insufficiency, surgical menopause, chronic glucocorticoid use). **If a clear endocrine disorder is identified, proceed to Step 2; otherwise, proceed to Step 4.**

## STEP 2 — Rule In / Rule Out
Determine whether testosterone measurement is being considered for diagnostic evaluation of nonspecific symptoms versus monitoring in known endocrine disease. **If the intent is diagnostic evaluation of nonspecific symptoms, proceed to Step 3; if for monitoring known disease, proceed to Step 4 (measurement may be appropriate).**

## STEP 3 — Classify or Stratify
Since no reliable correlation exists between symptoms and testosterone levels, classify the situation as "routine testosterone measurement not indicated for diagnosis." **Proceed to Step 4.**

## STEP 4 — Decide
Recommend against ordering testosterone test; instead, focus on evaluating other causes of symptoms and consider referral if endocrine pathology is suspected. **End.**

## Clinical Guardrails / Mimics / Pitfalls
Do not order testosterone solely based on nonspecific symptoms; avoid interpreting low testosterone as diagnostic of androgen deficiency syndrome; refrain from using testosterone levels to guide therapy in women with low libido without a confirmed diagnosis of hypoactive sexual desire disorder; be aware of assay variability and the lack of standardized reference ranges for women.

## Concrete Clinical Example
A 45‑year‑old woman reports fatigue and decreased libido for three months. She has regular menstrual cycles, no history of pituitary or adrenal disease, and is not taking glucocorticoids. The clinician considers ordering a testosterone level. Following the guideline, testosterone measurement is not recommended; instead, evaluate sleep, mood, medications, and consider HSDD assessment if appropriate.

**Source:** Androgen Therapy in Women: A Reappraisal: An Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline, Endocrine Society, 2014, DOI:10.1210/jc.2014-2260
